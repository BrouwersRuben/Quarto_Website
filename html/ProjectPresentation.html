The Game Quarto

Quarto is a board game invented by Swiss mathematician Blaise Müller in 1991.
The game is played on a 4x4 board with 16 pieces, each of which has four characteristics, either:

  • tall or short
  • green* or blue*
  • square or circular
  • hollow or solid

How the game is played

Each player takes a turn placing one piece on the board, but instead of having the option to select which piece to place on the board, each player has to place a piece that the opponent has selected for him.

Objective of the game

The aim of the game is to complete a line with four pieces that have the same characteristic.
The line may be vertical, horizontal or diagonal.
The winner is the player who places the last (fourth) piece on the board, completing a line.
